TACOS vs. ACOS: Why Total Ad Cost of Sale Is the Only Number That Matters

🦉
MarketOwl Team 5 min read

ACOS = Ad Spend ÷ Ad-Attributed Revenue
TACOS = Ad Spend ÷ Total Revenue (organic + ad-attributed)

The difference sounds simple, but the implications are significant.

The Organic Halo Effect

When your ads drive a sale, Amazon's algorithm takes notice. That sale contributes to your BSR, which influences your organic ranking, which drives more organic sales — which don't show up in your ACOS calculation at all.

A campaign with a 'bad' ACOS of 35% might actually be generating $3 of organic revenue for every $1 of ad-attributed revenue. Your true TACOS could be 9%. That's an excellent return — but ACOS alone would have you shutting the campaign down.

How to Calculate Your TACOS

  • Pull your total ad spend for the period
  • Pull your total revenue for the same period (all channels)
  • Divide spend by total revenue and multiply by 100

The Bottom Line

ACOS tells you how your ads perform in isolation. TACOS tells you how your advertising affects your entire business. Report on both, but make decisions based on TACOS.
